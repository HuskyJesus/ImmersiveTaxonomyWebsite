About the taxonomy

The Immersive Experience
Design Taxonomy

A practical framework for turning lessons, stories, exhibitions, trainings, and performances into intentional immersive experiences.

What it is

A map of ten design dimensions that appear across immersive experiences: interactivity, embodiment, co-participation, story, dynamics, gamification, immersive technology, meta-control, didactic capacity, and data.

Who it is for

Artists, educators, instructional designers, storytellers, researchers, trainers, performers, and immersive experience designers who want to name choices that are often made by instinct.

What it helps solve

It supports brainstorming, concept development, reviewing existing experiences, finding missing design opportunities, teaching immersive design, and refining intentional choices before production begins.

How designers use it

Choose a topic, then explore one element in each dimension. The result is not a score or a checklist; it is a design profile that clarifies what kind of experience you are building and why.

  • A map, not a checklist. Each dimension opens a family of design possibilities to consider, not boxes to tick.
  • Not a score. A higher element is not automatically better; Element 4 is not superior to Element 0.
  • Purpose decides. The right element in each dimension depends entirely on the purpose of the experience you are designing.
  • Technology is an amplifier. Immersive technology can deepen presence, but it is not the art itself.
