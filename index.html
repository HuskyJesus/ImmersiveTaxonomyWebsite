IXD Immersive Experience Design

A design methodology for immersive experiences

Design immersive experiences, one decision at a time

A guided framework for turning any topic — a lesson, a story, an exhibition, a training — into an immersive experience, by making deliberate design decisions across ten dimensions.

Start designing

Who it's for

Educators, designers, researchers, and storytellers — anyone who wants people to experience a subject rather than just hear about it. No background in immersive design is needed.

The problem it solves

Immersive experiences usually get designed by instinct: pick a technology, add a story, hope it works. Important decisions — how people participate, why they stay engaged, what they take away — get made by accident.

Why the taxonomy exists

The taxonomy names the ten decisions every immersive experience makes, and the options within each. This framework helps you design intentionally by choosing across each dimension instead of defaulting to habit.

When to use it

At the start of a project, in an ideation workshop, in the classroom while teaching experience design — or any time an idea feels flat and you want to see which design dimensions you haven't considered yet.
