About the taxonomy

What it is

The taxonomy is a map of ten design dimensions that appear across immersive experiences: interactivity, embodiment, co-participation, story, dynamics, gamification, immersive technology, meta-control, didactic capacity, and data.

Who it is for

Artists, educators, instructional designers, storytellers, researchers, trainers, performers, and immersive experience designers can use it to name choices that are often made by instinct.

What it helps solve

It supports brainstorming, concept development, reviewing existing experiences, finding missing design opportunities, teaching immersive design, and refining intentional choices before production begins.

How designers use it

Choose a topic, then explore one element in each dimension. The result is not a score or checklist; it is a design profile that helps clarify what kind of experience you are building and why.

  • A map, not a checklist. Each dimension opens a family of design possibilities.
  • Not a score. Every experience does not need the highest element; the right choice depends on the purpose.
  • Technology is an amplifier. Immersive technology can deepen presence, but it is not the art itself.

The Book

Based on the immersive experience design taxonomy developed by JJ Ruscella in "Immersion: The New Art Form - A Handbook for the Immersive Experience Designer."

The book frames immersive experience design as a new stage for artists, educators, performers, technologists, and subject-matter experts. It presents the taxonomy as a practical way to see opportunity: not a rigid rubric, but a language for building worlds people can enter, influence, remember, and learn from.
