The Immersive Experience
Design Taxonomy

Ten design dimensions. Five elements in each. One intentional way to turn any topic — a lesson, a story, an exhibition, a training — into an immersive experience.

Start Designing About the Taxonomy
  • A map, not a checklist. Each dimension lays out the possibilities for one design decision.
  • Not a score. Element 4 is not "better" than Element 0 — the right element depends on the purpose of the experience.
  • Technology amplifies immersion; it doesn't create it. Some of the most immersive experiences use no technology at all.

Based on the immersive experience design taxonomy developed by JJ Ruscella in “Immersion: The New Art Form — A Handbook for the Immersive Experience Designer.”

About the taxonomy

What it is

A framework that names the ten decisions every immersive experience makes — from how people interact and how present they feel, to how the story works and what the experience knows about its participants — and the five elements possible within each.

Who it's for

Educators, designers, researchers, and storytellers — anyone who wants people to experience a subject rather than just hear about it. No background in immersive design is needed.

The problem it solves

Immersive experiences usually get designed by instinct: pick a technology, add a story, hope it works. Important decisions — how people participate, why they stay engaged, what they take away — get made by accident instead of on purpose.

How it works

Enter your topic, then choose one element in each dimension that serves your purpose — or lock the choices you're sure of and randomize the rest as a brainstorming start. The generator turns your design profile into a complete experience concept you can refine and save.
